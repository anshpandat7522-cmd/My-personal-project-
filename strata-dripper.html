A four-minute ritual

The ring geometry is tuned to this sequence — rush it and the ribs do nothing for you.

0:00

Bloom

40g water, just off the boil. The rings hold the grounds level while gas escapes evenly.

0:45

First pour

Pour to 150g in slow concentric circles, tracing the ribs from center out.

1:30

Second pour

Top up to 300g. The stepped ridges keep flow steady without a paper filter's drag.

3:15

Drawdown

Bed drains flat, not cratered — a sign the rings did their job evenly.

Stoneware,
fired twice.

Each piece is thrown on a wheel, left to dry slow, bisque-fired, glazed by hand, then fired again to 1,250°C. No two rings turn out identical — that's the cone, not a flaw.
